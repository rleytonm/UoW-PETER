Benchmark suitability: topic modelling considerations

Flat topic modelling: 320 fine-grained taxonomy labels provide a rich flat label set. However, labels are highly imbalanced — "Intro to Neural Networks" has 92 lectures vs most with 1–5. Deep learning topics are over-represented relative to the overall NLP corpus scope. Use macro-F1 and per-class reporting.
Hierarchical topic modelling: The 3-level taxonomy tree is a strong fit. Parent IDs are encoded directly in topic IDs (e.g. 233 → 23 → 2), enabling clean hierarchical ground truth. ~9 root categories map naturally to coarse-grained levels.
Class imbalance: Topic distribution is long-tailed. Top 10 topics cover ~40% of labelled lectures. Rare topics may produce noisy evaluation signal.
Domain scope: NLP 56.5%, ML 19.2%, DL 10.9%, AI 7.5%, IR 5.8%. Algorithms will be stressed on NLP vocabulary. Cross-domain variant (LectureBankCD) available for broader testing.
Vocabulary: 1,221-term in-domain vocabulary extracted from slides. Useful as topic seed words or for coherence metrics (NPMI).
Scale: 7,499 records total; 1,352 with full annotations = usable benchmark set. Small by modern standards — suitable for focused NLP-domain evaluation or few-shot benchmarking.
